Week 4

Using Music21




The graphs above demonstrate various interesting infomration about the track Wet Hands by C418. The graphs each dictate a differing evaluation of the music, described below:

Note Quarter Length by Pitch
  • In music notation, notes are represented by symbols on a staff. The duration of a note is indicated by its shape, with a quarter note typically representing a quarter of a whole note.
  • The graph refers to the visual representation of data structure that shows the relationship between the pitch of notes and their duration in terms of quarter notes. In the case of the graph extracted from the song Wet Hands by C418, you can see long A pitches, with a wide spread of notes with massivly varying measure numbers.
Scatter plot of pitches
  • The graph dictates the variety of pitches present as well as the quater length.
  • It offers little more value than the Quarter length by pitch graph in my opinion
Music21 Graph
  • The graph indicates the count of notes within the Octave.
  • It is useful in investigating the most frequent notes in the piece.
